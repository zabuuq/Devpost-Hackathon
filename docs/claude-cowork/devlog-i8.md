# Less menu math, more space confetti

**A pile of small wins.** Iteration 8 is a polish bundle: ten quality-of-life and rule tweaks shipped together, plus a tutorial and screenshot refresh on top. None of these items reshape the game on their own, but stacked together they cut a real amount of friction out of every turn.

**Fewer clicks before the fun starts.** Fleet placement gets a Randomize button at the top of the ship list. Slap your whole fleet onto the grid in one go and start fighting. The probe rules got simpler too: every probe now costs 50 energy, no matter which ship launched it, and the Probe Ship's coverage bumped from a 6-by-6 to a 7-by-7 box so it actually feels different from the standard 4-by-4. When the killing blow lands on the opponent's last ship, the Victory screen barges in immediately instead of waiting for you to click End Turn.

**Sliders and labels stop fighting you.** Shield regen now auto-fills to a sensible repair value the first time a ship eats damage. Touch the slider once and the reactor takes the hint, even if you slide it back to zero. The Ship Panel's energy readout was renamed from "Energy after sliders" to "Energy after use" so it lands without naming the UI control. Escape now cancels probe, laser, and missile targeting, joining the move-preview Escape from a previous build. Shift+scroll horizontal pan got flipped to read the way most people expect.

**Less visual noise on the enemy.** Clicking a probed enemy ship now opens a stripped-down Ship Panel that shows only the name, shields, and armor. No more separator lines or empty Actions label crowding the view.

The How to Play overlay got a fresh sentence about Randomize, all 18 screenshots got regenerated to capture the new visuals, and the build is live now. Go bother your nemesis.
