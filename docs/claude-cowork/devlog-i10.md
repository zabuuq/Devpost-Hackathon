# The tab strip nobody loved

**Two panels at once, no swap.** The left side of the gameplay screen used to be a tab strip: Battle Log on one face, Ship Panel on the other, with an auto-swap that yanked the view to whichever one you'd just touched. Click a ship — Ship Panel appears. Deselect — Battle Log appears. Two pieces of information, one slot, and the game picked which one you got to see. That tab strip is gone. Ship Panel sits on top. Battle Log sits on the bottom, with a divider and a header so you remember it lives there. Both are visible the whole turn.

**Ship Panel is now your fleet, not just one hull.** The Ship Panel used to swallow its entire 2/3 of the column with sliders, stats, and four action buttons for the one ship you'd selected. Now it's a five-row accordion — Battleship, Probe Ship, Destroyer, Destroyer, Cruiser, in fleet order. Click a row to expand it; the expanded row holds the same dense single-ship view that used to dominate the panel. Clicking on the Command Grid expands the matching row. Clicking the row's header on the side menu pans the Command Grid to center on the ship. Destroyed ships dim out in place with a "(destroyed)" suffix, so you can still see who you used to have.

**Music waits for another day.** The plan included an ambient track. Sourcing one royalty-free track that doesn't sound like elevator music in space turns out to be its own thing. The toggle, the AudioManager hooks, and the GameState flag all came out — back into the backlog as a coherent restoration job for next time. SFX are still on.
