# Writing Voice — Battlestations: Nebula

Companion to `WRITING-STYLE.md`. That file covers mechanical rules (banned words, em dashes, voice rules). This file covers tone, character, and register for in-game and marketing copy.

## Name

**Gallows Deadpan**

## Description

Irreverent, chaotic, and dark, but clean enough for teenagers. The voice sounds like a world-weary narrator describing grim situations with the tone of a DMV clerk reading off a form: matter-of-fact, specific, and unimpressed with its own violence. It leans into genre-aware asides, mundane details dropped next to cosmic ones (a reconstituted turkey sandwich next to a hyperspace ambush), and rule-of-three lists where the third item undercuts the first two. It addresses the reader in second person as a participant, not a spectator.

No real profanity. Comical pseudo-swears are welcome and encouraged: *fudgesicles*, *shiitake mushrooms*, *mother puss bucket*, *son of a motherless goat*, *balderdash*, *dagnabbit*, *hobknocker*. Insults should sound unhinged but lexically safe.

Formality: low. Contractions always. Short punchy sentences interleaved with one longer sentence piling on concrete detail. Active voice. No corporate hedges ("may," "might consider," "it is recommended"). No therapy-speak. No em dashes.

Intended audience: teen and adult players who like dark comedy, tabletop games, and LitRPG-adjacent fiction. Think Dungeon Crawler Carl readers, Terry Pratchett fans, people who laughed at the flavor text in *Hades* and *Disco Elysium*.

What to avoid: sincere motivational language, earnest sci-fi grandeur, fourth-wall winks congratulating the reader, and any phrasing sounding like a tutorial written by HR.

## Writing samples

**Sample 1. Cold-open narration**

> One minute you were cruising through hyperspace, halfway through a reconstituted turkey sandwich and a mediocre opinion about the Oort Cloud. The next, something with all the subtlety of a cosmic sledgehammer dropped your fleet into an unmapped nebula and cut your sensors like a bored god flipping a circuit breaker.
>
> They came back online in pieces. Long enough to spot an enemy fleet drifting in the same cosmic dishwater you are. Long enough to confirm the obvious: somebody out here has live warheads, dead morals, and the patience of a hungry mimic.

**Sample 2. Mechanics explained in-voice**

> A probe is a flashlight in a haunted house. It costs energy, it lights up a small box of nebula, and for two turns you can see exactly which flavor of fudgesicle is about to ruin your afternoon. Then the lights go out and the nebula hands you a souvenir: a ghost marker, pinned to the map like a Post-it reading *a ship was here once, good luck, buddy*.
>
> You can fire on a ghost. The enemy moved three turns ago. Your shot hits empty space. Your log says miss. The nebula, somewhere, laughs.

**Sample 3. UI flavor copy, short form**

> Shields take priority when energy is tight. Drag the sliders, pick your poison, and accept that shiitake mushrooms, you probably allocated wrong. That's fine. Everyone allocates wrong. The ones who don't are lying or dead.
